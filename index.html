My undergraduate education was rooted in human biochemistry and fundamental laboratory techniques (e.g., protein purification, molecular cloning, etc.). In my senior year, I was exposed to the field of genomics through several seminars, and took computer science as an elective. I found these experiences led my interests to gravitate towards bioinformatics rather than traditional laboratory sciences. Therefore, I taught myself more computer programming before staring graduate school. Since then, I successfully transitioned to an interdisciplinary research group with a focus on statistical genetics and data analytics.

I hope to help usher in the era of precision medicine by identifying causal risk factors, diagnostic markers, and molecular profiles to stratify patients' disease risk.

If you are a business with Canadian offices, the Mitacs Accelerate program is an avenue for potential R&D internships. This is an initiative supported by the Canadian government that offers businesses access to graduate students with domain expertise, while giving students opportunities to apply their skills to solve real-life problems facing businesses. Projects run for a minimum of 4 months and costs are subsidized, so a business would pay only $7,500 per intern. Longer projects can be supported by a Mitacs Accelerate Fellowship for 18 months. If interested, check out their website or contact me for more details.
